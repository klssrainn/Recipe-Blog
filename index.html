Chocolate Crinkles Recipe

How many crinkles can this recipe make?

50 cookies

What do I need?

  • 1 cup natural unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon espresso powder (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup powdered sugar

How do I make it?

  1. Beat it.

    2. In the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with the paddle attachment (or beat by hand with a wooden spoon) beat together the cocoa powder, white sugar, and vegetable oil until it comes together into a shiny, gritty, black dough of sorts.

  2. Just beat it.

    3. Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing for 30 seconds each. Add the vanilla and beat in thoroughly.

  3. Dry ingredients.

    4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and espresso powder if using.

  4. Do not over-beat.

    5. Mix into the chocolate mixture on low speed until just combined. Do not over-beat.

  5. Chill.

    6. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and chill the dough for four hours or overnight.

  6. Preheat.

    7. Preheat the oven to 350°F and line two baking sheets with parchment paper. Place the powdered sugar in a wide bowl.

  7. Roll it.

    8. Using a rounded teaspoon, scoop out clumps of the chilled dough and roll them into 1-inch (2.5 cm) sized balls using your hands. Do several at a time and drop them into the powdered sugar as you go. The dough will be quite sticky, so it's useful to roll the dough in batches. Roll the balls in the powdered sugar and place on the cookie sheets spaced a few inches apart. (You should be able to get 12 to 16 on each sheet).

  8. Bake.

    9. Bake at 350°F for 10 to 13 minutes. Allow to cool a minute or two on the sheets before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Repeat with any remaining batter.
